Internet Pioneers

Vannevar Bush, D.Eng

Vannevar Bush (1980 - 1974) was an American engineer, inventor and science administrator that did not directly participate on the creation of the internet since he died before the world wide web was invented. Even so, on his essay, "As We May Think" he described the theory of a machine that could store and retrieve documents. He called this machine "memex".


Doug Englebart, Ph.D.

Douglas Carl Engelbart (1925 - 2013) was an American engineer and inventor and a computer and internet pioneer. He is best known for his work on the interaction between human and computer. He is known for inventing the mouse, the development of the hypertext, computer networks and the early principles of GUI. Douglas saw technology as a way to augment human intellect.


Ted Nelson, Ph.D.

Theodor Nelson is an American pioneer of information technology, philosopher and sociologist. He is the one that came up with the term of hypertext and hypermedia. He considered a controversial figure in the computer world because he has come with so many ideas but never completes any projects


Steve Jobs

Steve Jobs (1955 - 2011) Was an american inventor and industrial designer, better known to be one of the founder of Apple.inc, founder of neXT and moajority shareholder of Pixar Studios. Steve Jobs is widely considered as one of the most important pioneers of the microcomputer revolution.

Tim Bernes-Lee, KBE

Tim Bernes-Lee is a english engineer and computer scientist that work under CERN. He is known for his work on inventing the World Wide Web and for implementing the first sucessful communication between a HTTP client and a server using the internet and being the current director of the W3C.Tim is one of only six inductees in the first World Wide Web Hall of Fame.

Marc Andreessen / Eric Bina

Marc Andreessen is an american software engineering. He is known for being the co-author for Mosaic that became the first widely used web browser, and later he co-founded Netscape. His aim for browsers was to make them more user friendly and also way more graphically rich which is why Mosaic and Netscape were so popular. Marc Andreessen was also one of only six inductees in the first World Wide Web Hall of Fame.

Rob Hartill, Ph.D.

Rob Hartill is a computer engineer and web designer. He is widely known for his work on the internet movie Database website and his work on the Apache web server. He is considered as one of the pioneers of the World Wide Web.


Lou Montulli

Lou Montulli is a programmer very well known for his work producing web browsers. His most popular work is the web browser Lynx that until today is still used making it the oldest web browser. Lou Montulli is considered as one of the pioneers for the development of the World Wide Web. He is also one of teh six inductees in the first World Wiede Web Hall of Fame.

Vint Cerf

Vint Cerf is an American internet pioneer, considered as one of the fathers of the internet. He is widely known for his development on TCP and IP protocols and the whole architecture of the internet. He has been Honored with The National Medal of Technology, the Turing Award, the Presidential Medal of Freedom and the Marconi Prize. Cerf is also and incutee in the Hall of Fame


Bob Kahn

Robert Kahn is an American electrical engineering that helped produce the web protocols known as TCP and IP. He is considered as one of the fathers of the internet. He is responsible for originating DARPA's internet program. He showed the ARPNET by connecting up to 20 computers.
