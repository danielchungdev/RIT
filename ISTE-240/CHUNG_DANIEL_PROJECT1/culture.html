CULTURE

Panamanian culture is a mix of cultures all over the world. Panama has mixed from Native, Europeans thanks to the Spanish Conquistadors, Asian, American and African because of the Panama’s Canal project. Panama values culture a lot and this is seen throughout the many festivals that are held throughout the year.

Panamanian Cuisine

Panama’s cuisine is a big mix of cultures. Flavorwise it’s usually mild, almost no spicy food is found among traditional foods. Common ingredients are rice, white, wheat, flour, plantains, yuca, chicken, meat, pork and seafood. Panama is well known for “Ceviche” and “Patacones” which are must tries when visiting.

Panamanian music

Panama’s traditional music is very influenced by the Native Americans and are usually heavy on percussion. It’s a mix of spanish cumbia, salsa, Jamaican Reggae. However, modern Panamanian songs are heavily based on a variant of Reggae called Reggaeton also known as “Plena” in Panama. Famous Panamanian artists include Ruben Blades considered best Salsa singer, Joey Montana for his songs “Picky” and “Hola”, Erika Ender for helping compose world hit “Despacito” and Sech for his most recent latin hit “Otro Trago”.

Panamanian Visual Arts

Panama’s visual arts are heavily influenced the Native Americans known as “Kuna” on their “Molas” which are handmade colorful portraits made with textiles. These can be used either as clothing or as murals. Most Kuna’s wear them while people in the city use them as decorations.
